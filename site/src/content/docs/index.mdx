# Your team’s AI harness <span class="accent">drifts</span>. Pin it.

<p class="lead">Every dev hand-edits their own <code>settings.json</code>, deny lists, hooks, skills and agents. They go stale, silently. agent-rigger makes the harness a <strong>versioned package</strong>: one catalog, one command, zero drift.</p>

<h2 class="ar-h2">Setups drift. Nobody notices until it breaks.</h2>
<p class="ar-sub">The harness is config sprawl — and config sprawl rots in the dark.</p>

<div class="ar-grid">
  <div class="ar-card">
    <span class="num">01 — MANUAL</span>
    <h3>Installed by hand</h3>
    <p>Each machine gets its guardrails, hooks and skills copied in by hand. No source of truth, no record of what landed.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ar-card">
    <span class="num">02 — INCONSISTENT</span>

  </div>
  <div class="ar-card">

<section>

<h2 class="ar-h2">Catalog in, rig out.</h2>
<p class="ar-sub">A shared catalog says what should be installed. A manifest records what is. <code>check</code> tells you the difference.</p>

<div class="ar-grid">
  <div class="ar-card">
    <span class="num">CATALOG</span>
    <h3>The team decision</h3>
    <p>A versioned content repo lists the artifacts and packs your team agreed on. The engine ships <strong>no content of its own</strong> — zero opinion baked in.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ar-card">
    <span class="num">INSTALL</span>
    <h3>One command, a plan first</h3>
    <p><code>install</code> resolves packs and dependencies, shows a terraform-style plan, backs up every file, and never writes without confirmation.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ar-card">
    <span class="num">CHECK</span>
    <h3>Drift, caught</h3>
    <p>Put <code>check</code> in CI or a machine hook. Exit 0 means every guardrail and context entry matches the catalog. Anything else is drift.</p>
  </div>
</div>

</section>
